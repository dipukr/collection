Software architecture is the high-level, strategic blueprint of a system, defining its overall structure, core components, and their interactions to meet business needs, while design is the tactical, low-level process of detailing how individual components will be built and work together. Think of architecture as defining the "what" and "why" (e.g., using a microservices pattern for scalability), whereas design is the "how" of specific implementation, like choosing a particular design pattern (e.g., the Strategy pattern) to solve a problem within one of those services.

Software architecture:
  • Focus: Strategic, big-picture decisions and the "why".
  • Scope: The entire system, including its core structure, high-level component interactions, and fundamental principles.
  • Key decisions:
    • Choosing an overall pattern like microservices or serverless.
    • Addressing non-functional requirements like security, performance, and scalability across the system.
    • Defining the interactions between major components and systems.
    Software design:
    • Using specific design patterns, like the Singleton pattern, to solve a concrete problem within a component.
    • Structuring the internal logic and behavior of a module.
    • Defining data structures and interfaces for a specific part of the system.
